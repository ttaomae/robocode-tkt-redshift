About RedShift

RedShift is a Robocode AdvancedRobot. It is primarily designed for one one one battles.The following outlines its basic strategy.

Movement

RedShift attempts to always move and always stay perpendicular to the enemy. This strategy works well against head-on targeting. In addition, RedShift will also try to move closer to the enemy if it is too far or move farther if it is too close. Being closer to the target makes it easier to hit the target. However, this also means that the enemy will be able to hit you easier. The distance was chosen to try to balance these two factors.

Targeting and Firing

RedShift uses linear targeting. Linear targeting assumes that the target will continue to move in the same direction with the same velocity and aims where the robot would be by the time your bullet reaches them. Since not all robots will follow this behavior, I keep track of multiple past velocities and use a weighted moving average of these velocities rather than just the most recently scanned velocity. RedShift will fire at every opportunity that it can as long as it is not too far from the enemy. The firing power is inversely proportional to the distance from the target. As the distance from the target increases, the power decreases.

Vulnerabilities

Currently, RedShift is vulnerable to other linear targeting robots since it constantly moves in approximately the same direction. It also performs relatively poorly in melee battles. This is because much of the strategy assumes that there is only one other robot and it makes to adjustments when there are more robots.
